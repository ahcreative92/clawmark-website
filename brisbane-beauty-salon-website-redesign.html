What a mobile-first redesign actually changes for a beauty business.

Most Brisbane beauty salons still run websites built for desktop screens. Over 70% of their customers are searching on a phone. That gap costs bookings every single day.

A Fortitude Valley beauty salon came to us with a website that hadn't been touched since 2019. Good business, loyal clients, solid reviews. But their site was losing them new bookings every day — and they didn't know it.

The site that was working against them

The old site looked fine on a desktop. A homepage with some service photos, a price list as a PDF download, a contact form, and a booking link buried at the bottom. It had been good enough when it was built. But that was a different internet.

Today, over 70% of searches for beauty salons in Brisbane happen on a mobile phone. And when those customers landed on this salon's site, here's what they got: text too small to read without zooming, a layout that required horizontal scrolling, a PDF price list that wouldn't open on half the devices, and a booking button that was cut off by the screen edge.

Most of them left within 10 seconds. The salon never knew those visitors existed.

72% of beauty salon searches in Brisbane now happen on mobile
8 sec is the average time a mobile visitor waits before bouncing from a broken layout
3× more bookings on average when the booking button is visible above the fold on mobile

What we changed — and why

The brief was simple: the site needed to work for the customer who finds it on Instagram at 9pm on a Thursday and wants to book Saturday morning. That customer is on a phone, in a hurry, and will not scroll through four paragraphs of history to find the booking link.

Mobile-first layout

We rebuilt the entire site in Webflow with a mobile-first approach. That means the design starts from the smallest screen and scales up — not the other way around. Every element was tested on iPhone and Android before it was finalised. The booking button went into the hero section, above the fold, on every page.

Services as a browsable menu, not a PDF

The PDF price list was replaced with a clean, filterable services section. Customers can tap a category — nails, lashes, skin, brows — and see only what's relevant to them. Each service shows the price, time, and a direct booking link. No hunting. No downloading.

Photography that actually converts

The old site had four photos taken on a DSLR in 2018. We reorganised the existing photos into a gallery that loads fast on mobile and shows the quality of the work immediately. The photos didn't change. The presentation did.

Brisbane Example: This Fortitude Valley salon saw their online booking rate increase by 40% in the first month after the new site launched — without any advertising spend. The difference was entirely in the user experience on mobile.

How 48 hours is actually possible

The question we get asked most is: how do you build a website in 48 hours without it being a template that looks like everyone else's?

The answer is process. We've built sites for beauty salons, trades, fitness businesses, and hospitality across Brisbane. We know what works for each industry. That knowledge means we don't start from scratch on strategy — we start with a proven structure and adapt it to the specific business.

For this salon, the structure was: hero with booking CTA → services menu → gallery → about → testimonials → second CTA. That layout converts. We've seen it work across dozens of sites. What made this salon's site theirs was the photography, the brand colours, the copy, and the specific services they offer.

Webflow gives us the speed. Our process gives us the consistency. The result is a site that's both fast to build and genuinely tailored to the business.

"The best websites for small businesses aren't impressive — they're invisible. The customer finds what they need, books, and moves on. That's the goal."

What this means for your beauty business

If your website was built more than three years ago, it's almost certainly hurting you on mobile. Not because it's ugly — but because it wasn't designed for the way people search and book now.

A few things worth checking right now:

  • Open your website on your phone. Not your laptop. Your phone. Can you find the booking link without scrolling? Can you read the price list without zooming?
  • Check your Google Analytics (or ask us to). What percentage of your visitors are on mobile? For most Brisbane salons it's above 65%.
  • Time how long it takes your site to load on your phone. If it's more than 3 seconds on a 4G connection, you're losing customers before the page even appears.

None of this requires a full rebuild if the fundamentals are right. But if they're not — if your site is costing you bookings month after month — fixing it is not as expensive or complicated as most salon owners assume.
